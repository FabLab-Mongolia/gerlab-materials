# Storage, Labeling, and Waste

[← Previous: Vacuum-Forming Materials](vacuum-forming-materials.md) · [↑ Materials Library](README.md) · [Next: Test Records →](test-records.md)

## Minimum Label

- Exact material/product
- Manufacturer or supplier
- Grade, formulation, or product code
- Dimensions/diameter/thickness
- Date received/opened and expiry where relevant
- Owner/project if not common stock
- Hazard or storage requirement
- Status: Approved / Conditional / Test only / Quarantine

Never rely on an unlabeled offcut's appearance.

## Storage Principles

- Protect sheet and stock from moisture, sunlight, heat, dirt, corrosion, bending, and impact.
- Store long/heavy stock so it cannot roll, fall, or block exits.
- Keep filament dry and identified.
- Keep resin, ink, solvent, paint, and adhesive closed, contained, and stored by SDS compatibility.
- Separate flammables, oxidizers, reactive materials, batteries, and ignition sources as required.
- Use first-in/first-out rotation and inspect older stock before use.

## Quarantine

Move unknown, damaged, contaminated, expired, leaking, corroded, swollen, mixed, or disputed material to a marked quarantine area. Record why it was quarantined and who may release or dispose of it.

## Offcuts and Reuse

Retain offcuts only when identity and useful dimensions remain clear. Mark both sides or keep them in a dedicated labeled bin. Do not mix similar-looking plastics or alloys.

## Waste Streams

Separate at minimum:

- Clean recyclable stock/offcuts by known material
- General nonhazardous waste
- Sharp metal/blade waste
- Uncured resin and resin-contaminated waste
- Ink, paint, adhesive, solvent, and chemical waste
- Batteries and electronic waste
- Dust, filter, and contaminated extraction waste

Do not drain, burn, melt, evaporate, cure, mix, or dispose of process waste without an approved route. Follow [GerLab Safety](https://github.com/FabLab-Mongolia/gerlab-safety) and local requirements.

## Inventory Review

Periodically verify labels, SDS/TDS links, condition, quantity, expiry, approved status, storage compatibility, and waste-container capacity.
