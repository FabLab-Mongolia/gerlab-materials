# CNC Materials

## CNC Router Candidates

- Verified plywood, MDF, and timber
- Machinable plastics such as approved acrylic, HDPE, or acetal
- Aluminum or other nonferrous stock only with confirmed machine capability, tooling, workholding, and parameters
- Modeling board, foam, or composite only after dust/emission review

## CNC Mill and Desktop Mill Candidates

- Aluminum alloys appropriate to the task
- Mild steel, stainless, brass, copper, or engineering plastic only within verified machine/tool capability
- Machinable wax and approved prototyping stock
- PCB material only with suitable dust control and board identification

## Required Stock Information

- Material family and grade/alloy
- Dimensions and condition
- Heat treatment or hardness where relevant
- Coating, laminate, reinforcement, or hidden fasteners
- Flatness, straightness, and surface contamination
- Supplier documentation and intended final use

## Restricted Materials

- Unknown alloy, plastic, board, laminate, or composite
- Stock with nails, screws, staples, wire, stone, dirt, voids, or hidden inclusions
- Asbestos-containing, lead-painted, treated, contaminated, or hazardous reclaimed material
- Hardened material beyond verified tooling/capacity
- Round or irregular stock without safe workholding
- Material too small, thin, warped, or damaged to secure reliably

## Machining Considerations

Approval must consider tool material/geometry, chip load, speed, feed, depth, coolant/lubrication, heat, chip control, dust extraction, workholding, and finish. Wood species, plywood glue, recycled plastic, and nominally identical alloys may behave differently.

Record actual results rather than describing a material simply as “wood,” “plastic,” or “metal.”
