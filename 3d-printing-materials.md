## FDM Filament

| Material | Typical strengths | Main considerations |
|---|---|---|
| PLA | Easy printing, detail, low warping | Limited heat resistance; brittle grades; not automatically food safe |
| PETG | Tough, practical parts, chemical/moisture resistance | Stringing; strong bed adhesion; dry storage helpful |
| TPU/flexible | Flexible, impact resistant, grips and seals | Slow controlled feed; hardness matters; moisture sensitive |
| ABS/ASA | Tough, heat-resistant functional parts | Enclosure and ventilation; warping; emissions; staff approval |
| Nylon/PA | Strong, wear resistant, functional parts | Highly moisture sensitive; drying and suitable bed/nozzle required |
| PC | Heat and impact resistance | High-temperature process; warping; machine/material approval required |
| PP | Light, fatigue and chemical resistance | Difficult adhesion; dedicated surface/process may be needed |
| Filled/composite | Stiffness, dimensional stability, appearance | Abrasive; hardened nozzle often required; fiber/dust controls |
| PVA/BVOH | Dissolvable supports | Strong moisture sensitivity; compatibility and drying required |

Never copy temperatures blindly. Use the filament manufacturer's data, printer/nozzle limits, build-surface guidance, and a recorded test.

## Filament Identification and Storage

Label every spool with polymer, brand, color, diameter, lot, opened date, and drying history. Store moisture-sensitive filament sealed with desiccant. Quarantine tangled, contaminated, brittle, swollen, or unknown filament.

## SLA Resin

- Use only resin confirmed compatible with the specific Formlabs printer and tank/platform system.
- Keep product, version, color, lot, expiry, and cartridge/container identity traceable.
- Consult the SDS for handling and the TDS for print/post-cure requirements.
- Wear compatible gloves for uncured resin and contaminated parts.
- Do not mix resins unless the manufacturer explicitly permits it.
- Keep resin waste, wash solvent, supports, wipes, and failed prints in the approved waste route.
- Biocompatible or skin-contact claims apply only when the exact validated workflow and post-cure are followed.

## Performance Claims

A printed polymer is anisotropic and process-dependent. Do not assume bulk-material strength, pressure safety, food safety, electrical insulation, chemical resistance, UV life, or temperature rating without application-specific verification.
