# Vinyl and Print Media

[← Previous: CNC Materials](cnc-materials.md) · [↑ Materials Library](README.md) · [Next: Vacuum-Forming Materials →](vacuum-forming-materials.md)

## Vinyl Cutter Media

- Calendered or cast self-adhesive vinyl approved for the cutter
- Stencil and masking film
- Heat-transfer film where the full transfer process is approved
- Paper/card and specialty media within blade/force limits
- Transfer tape matched to the face film and application

## Vinyl Printer Media

- Printer/ink-compatible printable vinyl
- Banner, paper, film, canvas, or specialty media listed for the printer
- Matching laminate and overlaminate where durability is required
- Compatible take-up, heater, and drying conditions

## Record These Details

- Manufacturer, product code, color, lot, and roll width
- Face material, adhesive type, liner, and thickness
- Indoor/outdoor rating and expected life
- Ink compatibility, heater/drying requirements, and profile
- Laminate and transfer-tape pairing
- Surface preparation and application temperature
- Cut force, blade, offset, speed, print profile, and curing/outgassing time

## Restrictions

- Do not put cutter/print vinyl into the laser cutter. Vinyl commonly contains PVC and laser approval is a separate decision.
- Do not print unknown media; coatings can damage the printer or produce poor/unsafe emissions.
- Quarantine curled, wet, contaminated, expired, poorly stored, or delaminating rolls.
- Do not promise vehicle, floor, outdoor, food-contact, or skin-contact performance without the product's verified rating and correct application system.

## Storage

Store rolls upright or as recommended, protected from heat, sunlight, moisture, dust, crushing, and sharp bends. Retain labels and avoid mixing unidentified offcuts.
