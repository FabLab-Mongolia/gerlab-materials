# Vacuum-Forming Materials

[← Previous: Vinyl and Print Media](vinyl-print-materials.md) · [↑ Materials Library](README.md) · [Next: Storage, Labeling, and Waste →](storage-labeling-waste.md)

Vacuum forming heats thermoplastic sheet and draws it over a mold. Sheet identity, thickness, forming range, drying, mold design, and trimming method all affect results.

## Common Candidate Sheets

- HIPS: common prototyping and packaging sheet
- ABS: tougher formed parts with appropriate heating and ventilation
- PETG: clear or impact-resistant forms; grade and drying needs must be checked
- Acrylic/PMMA: appearance applications; forming conditions and brittleness require control
- Polycarbonate: high-performance sheet requiring verified high-temperature processing
- Other thermoplastic sheet only with manufacturer and Formech guidance

Formech supplies HIPS, ABS, and PETG among its common forming materials, but GerLab must verify the exact 508FS configuration and local stock.

## Sheet Record

- Polymer and exact grade
- Manufacturer/product
- Color and surface finish
- Thickness and sheet dimensions
- Protective film and which side faces the mold
- Drying/preconditioning requirements
- Forming-temperature or heating guidance
- Food/contact or fire-rating claims, if relevant
- Recycled content and batch variability

## Mold Material Considerations

Molds must tolerate heat and vacuum load, have suitable draft, smooth edges, ventilation holes, and safe release. Wood, MDF, plaster, resin, printed polymer, modeling board, or metal molds require separate approval based on temperature, strength, moisture, sealant, and surface finish.

## Restrictions

- Unknown plastic sheet
- Thermoset plastic that will not reform
- Sheet with unknown coating, adhesive, contamination, or incompatible protective film
- Material that releases hazardous fumes at forming temperature
- Sheet outside clamp-frame dimensions or verified thickness range
- Mold that may collapse, trap the part, puncture sheet, or damage the machine

## Test Record

Record heater zones/time, sheet temperature where measured, sag behavior, mold temperature, vacuum response, cooling time, webbing, thinning, detail, release, and trimming result.
