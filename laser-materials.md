# Laser Materials

[← Previous: Compatibility Matrix](machine-compatibility.md) · [↑ Materials Library](README.md) · [Next: 3D-Printing Materials →](3d-printing-materials.md)

GerLab uses an enclosed Epilog Fusion Pro CO₂ laser. Material identity and local approval are required before every new product or formulation.

## Common Candidate Materials

These may be suitable after verification:

- Acrylic/PMMA designed for laser processing
- Uncoated natural wood and verified plywood
- Paper, card, and corrugated cardboard under fire-controlled testing
- Approved textiles, leather, rubber, cork, or foam with known composition
- Anodized/coated metal for marking where the machine/process supports it

Candidate does not mean approved. Adhesives, coatings, flame retardants, fillers, and laminate layers can change behavior.

## Prohibited or Restricted

- **PVC, vinyl, and materials containing chlorine:** prohibited for laser cutting/engraving.
- Unknown plastic, foam, rubber, artificial leather, laminate, or composite: prohibited until identified.
- Polycarbonate: poor/discolored CO₂ cutting behavior; staff approval and manufacturer guidance required.
- ABS and other high-smoke or problematic polymers: not approved unless GerLab completes a specific review.
- Fiberglass, carbon-fiber composite, PTFE/fluoropolymer, halogenated polymer, or hazardous coating: prohibited unless an authoritative process-specific approval exists.
- Pressurized, sealed, reflective, explosive, battery-containing, or contaminated items: prohibited.
- Metal cutting: do not assume the CO₂ laser can cut metal.

## Identification Questions

- What is the base material and exact trade name?
- Does it contain PVC/vinyl, chlorine, fluorine, bromine, flame retardant, adhesive, paint, or unknown coating?
- Is there an SDS/TDS or manufacturer laser-processing statement?
- Is the thickness within verified capability?
- What fumes, residue, flame, or lens contamination could occur?

## Test Requirements

Use a small supervised sample, extraction and Air Assist, conservative settings, and continuous observation. Stop for sustained flame, unusual odor, dense/colored smoke, corrosive deposit, melting, bubbling, or unexpected residue.

Record verified results in [Material Test Records](test-records.md). Online settings from another machine or wattage are starting references only.
