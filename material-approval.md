# Material Approval

[↑ Materials Library](README.md) · [Next: Compatibility Matrix →](machine-compatibility.md)

## Information Required

Before approving a new material, record:

- Product and manufacturer
- Exact material family and grade
- Supplier and batch/lot where relevant
- Thickness, diameter, or stock dimensions
- Coating, adhesive, backing, filler, reinforcement, or surface treatment
- SDS and Technical Data Sheet (TDS), where applicable
- Intended machine and process
- Expected emissions, chips, heat, fire, chemical, and waste hazards
- Required tooling, settings, ventilation, PPE, and post-processing
- Final-part performance requirements

## Approval Workflow

1. Identify the material from reliable documentation—not color, smell, or appearance.
2. Screen for prohibited ingredients and incompatible processes.
3. Confirm the machine, tooling, bed, nozzle, blade, cutter, ink, or heater can accept it.
4. Review GerLab safety controls and waste route.
5. Define a small supervised test with conservative settings.
6. Observe flame, smoke, odor, melting, warping, tool load, adhesion, finish, and residue.
7. Record results in [Material Test Records](test-records.md).
8. Approve, restrict, reject, or request more evidence.

## Automatic Rejection or Quarantine

- Unknown or unlabeled composition
- Missing supplier information where composition matters
- Mixed scrap or reclaimed material with uncertain contents
- Hidden coatings, adhesives, films, pressure, liquid, batteries, or fasteners
- Damaged, wet, contaminated, degraded, swollen, corroded, or expired stock
- Material exceeding machine dimensions or verified capacity

## Change Control

A previous approval does not automatically cover a new supplier, formulation, coating, thickness, resin version, filament blend, adhesive, or recycled batch. Recheck when any of these change.
